01
Essence

The window is the work.

Scenic Windows manufactures precision-engineered aluminum window systems for North American residential architecture. We make engineered objects, and we let them be the hero. The brand is the frame around the frame.

Engineered

Every claim is a measurable spec. Tolerances, U-values, thermal break depth, locking points. We earn the words before we use them.

Performance-Led

We talk about what the window does, not how it feels in the showroom. Comfort, security, and energy are outcomes — never adjectives.

Confident

Short tagline. Period. No qualifiers, no hedging, no excitement marks. The product is enough; the copy stays out of its way.

Specific

Numbers, units, materials, mechanisms. 34 mm polyamide beats "advanced thermal." The detail is the design.

Manifesto

One window.
Three modes. No compromise.

We were built around an idea that's older than most marketing categories: a window can be precise, beautiful, and quiet — and you shouldn't have to pick two. The tilt-turn is the proof. Polyamide is the proof. The frame finished in anthracite, sitting flush in the wall of a North American hillside residence, holding back forty degrees of weather without making a sound — that's the proof.

Everything in this book — the typography, the brass rule above the eyebrow, the periods at the end of every tagline — exists to keep that proof visible.

08
Voice

How we write.

The brochure writes like a product engineer with a tagline writer over their shoulder: every claim is a measurable spec, dressed up in tight, punchy phrasing. The body explains why the feature matters, in one sentence.

01

Engineered

Every claim earns its place by referencing a mechanism — polyamide break, multi-point lock, profile geometry. We write from the inside out.

02

Performance-led

Outcomes over adjectives. "Reduces heat transfer" — not "premium thermal experience." If you can't measure it, don't claim it.

03

Confident

Short tagline. Period. No qualifiers, no superlatives, no exclamation marks. The product carries the weight; the copy steps aside.

04

Specific

Digits, units, materials. 34 mm, ≤ 1.4 W/m²K, 48 hours. Use the non-breaking space between value and unit. Always.

Mechanics

Six rules that hold the voice together.

01 — First-Person Plural

"Our advanced RPT design uses…" — never second-person, except in CTAs ("Request a quote").

02 — Headlines Come in Pairs

A short tagline ("ENGINEERED FOR NORTH AMERICAN CLIMATES. BUILT TO LAST.") followed by a descriptive line. The tagline does the work; the line below adds dimension.

03 — Periods, Not Exclamations

End every headline phrase with a period — even single-word ones. Never use exclamation marks. Em dashes break up technical descriptions.

04 — Numbers Are Digits

"34 mm polyamide", "≤ 1.4 W/m²K", "48 hours". Always include units. Always use the non-breaking space between value and unit.

05 — Acronyms Spelled, Then Short

"Reduced Pressure Technology (RPT)" on first use. After that, "RPT". Same rule for U-value, RAL, AAMA, ENERGY STAR®.

06 — No Emoji. Ever.

Not in marketing, not in product, not in social, not in commit logs. The brand expresses warmth through brass and Playfair italic — not through symbols.

Before · After

Same idea, two ways to write it.

Off-Voice
"Premium luxury windows you'll love."
Adjective-stacked. Second-person. No proof.
On-Voice
"Engineered for comfort. Built to last."
Two outcomes, two periods. The proof lives in the spec below.
Off-Voice
"Stay warmer in winter with our amazing technology!"
Excited. Vague. Reaches for the reader.
On-Voice
"Polyamide thermal break reduces heat transfer and improves insulation."
Mechanism · outcome · outcome. One sentence, no decoration.

Iconography

Brass circles & dashed hinges.

The brand uses two icon vocabularies. Feature icons sit inside a 44–48 px brass circle. Mode diagrams are line drawings of the window itself, with brass dashed lines indicating hinge direction. Both are drawn at 24 px with a 1.5 px stroke and square caps.

Motion

Restrained, never theatrical.

The brand is engineered, not playful. Three durations cover most cases. Standard ease for everything except modal entrances. No bounces, no springs, no scale-on-hover.

140 ms
Fast — Interaction

Button hover, link hover, color transitions. Standard ease. The user shouldn't notice the animation — only that it feels right.

240 ms
Base — Reveal

Section fade-in on scroll, card promotion, accordion expansion. Standard ease.

420 ms
Slow — Emphasis

Quote sheet slide-over, modal entry, page transitions. Emphasis ease for entrances, standard ease for exits.

Standard Ease
cubic-bezier(0.22, 0.61, 0.36, 1)
Default for hover, fade, color transitions.
Emphasis Ease
cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)
Used for modal entrances and significant reveals only.
